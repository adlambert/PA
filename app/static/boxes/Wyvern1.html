Designed by architects Messrs Harrington,Ley Kirkham & Partners of Bishopsgate Without, London. This grand building stands out proudly above the surrounding terraces. Originally a school for girls and infants. This view taken from undeveloped land at the north end of Peel Road, you can see the children in the bottom left of the photo are only visible from the waist up. This was how the area was landscaped until the new houses were built on Peel Road in the 1980s. A metal building was put up on this land in the 1930s, acting as an extension to the school, with sports facilities including a long jump sandpit and tennis courts. The metal building was used by the Ministry of Food in WWII, dealing with ration books, and a robust air raid shelter was added for the staff.
