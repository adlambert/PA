Looking West along Stratford Road from the Anson Road junction. The long building on the north side of Stratford Road is part of McCorquodale printers who set up a factory in Wolverton following an arrangement between Richard Moon of the railway company and George McCorquodale to provide a ready workforce for the printing company and employment for the unmarried young ladies of the area. Buildings were continually added and updated on both sides of the Stratford Road from 1878, at its peak "The Print" employed around a thousand people.
This building was demolished in 1988 and replaced by car showrooms and forecourts.
